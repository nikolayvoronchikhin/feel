Pebble watch-face to check comfort-level of current weather conditions.

feel is a simple weather-status watchface. Useful for active atheletes, meteorologists, weather nerds, and anyone who wants to know why the weather has 'felt' a certain way.

Often times we perceive the weather based on how hot or how cold the temperature is, but certain environmental conditions make the temperature feel different. In particular, the dew-point is an indicator of how saturated the air is with water vapour.

For instance, on a typical summer day in San Francisco, the temperature is 21C (70F) with a dew-point of 14C (57F). Feels comfortable. Compare this to New York, with a temperature of 29C (84F) and dew-point of 21C (70F). Rather uncomfortable, isn't it?

  • If the air is saturated, then less evaporation from the skin will have occurred, and the air feels 'heavier'.
  • If the air isn't saturated as much, more evaporation from the skin will have occurred, and the air feels 'lighter'.

feel takes the weather data, courtesy of OpenWeatherMap and extracts the temperature and relative humidity. Through a rather complicated formula, the dew-point is computed using the temperature and relative humidity. Use it for a week and learn something about how the weather works.
